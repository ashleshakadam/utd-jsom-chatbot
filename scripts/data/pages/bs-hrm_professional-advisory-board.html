    Professional Advisory Board – BS in Human Resource Management

    Guided by HR and Business Leaders

    At UT Dallas, our Human Resource Management program benefits from the insights of an Advisory Board made up of top HR executives, business leaders and industry experts. Representing leading companies across multiple sectors, these professionals—including Chief Human Resource Officers, Chief Operating Officers and General Counsels—help shape our curriculum to ensure it reflects real-world business challenges. Their guidance equips you with the knowledge, skills and strategic mindset needed to thrive in HR and beyond.

    Our Advisory Board for the BS-HRM Program

    Jay Altizer

    Chief Operating Officer
    Fourth

    Read Bio

    Bio:

    Jay Altizer is the Chief Operating Officer of Fourth, a leading provider of labor management, inventory management and payroll software for the restaurant and hospitality industry. Jay has over 30 years of experience leading businesses in the software, business services and CPG industries. He has extensive private equity experience and currently serves on the board of The Expo Group, a New State Capital portfolio company.

    Prior to Fourth, Jay was the President of GES, a global event services provider with $1.15 billion in sales and 3,500 full-time employees across North America, the UK, and Europe. Previously, he served as SVP and General Manager at the Morningstar Foods division of Dean Foods. Jay began his career in professional services at Deloitte Consulting, later working at Bain & Company, where he led strategy, M&A and transformation efforts for global clients.

    Jay holds two bachelor’s degrees from Virginia Tech and an MBA from the Tuck School of Business at Dartmouth College. He lives in Dallas with his wife and three children.

    Leann Day

    VP of Human Resources
    Aimbridge Hospitality

    Read Bio

    Bio:

    Leann has built a 15+ year career in human resources, working across a variety of industries including financial services, life sciences consulting, real estate, restaurants, retail and hospitality. She has experience in Fortune 500 companies, publicly traded corporations, private family-owned businesses, mid-sized firms, and start-ups within companies such as Whataburger, Kemper Insurance, EDS and Tandy Leather.

    Leann is a strategic HR leader known for being a trusted advisor, sounding board, and “thought challenger” to operational leadership. Her priority is understanding the business first to align HR functions with key objectives, allowing her to design and implement innovative HR strategies that drive organizational performance, talent development and operational efficiency. She is committed to ensuring HR is recognized as a strategic business partner with a “seat at the table.”

    Passionate about company culture, Leann considers herself a “culture fanatic,” believing that HR plays a crucial role in fostering engagement, productivity, and inclusivity through everyday actions. She understands that company culture is built by every individual within an organization and is not merely defined by words on paper.

    On the administrative side of HR, Leann values efficiency, meaningful compensation programs tied to business results, and impactful health and wellness initiatives. Notably, she established the Tandy Leather Employee Foundation, a 501(c)(3) providing financial assistance to employees facing hardships.

    Throughout her career, Leann has built high-performing HR teams and leadership development programs while continuously learning from others and sharing her expertise. She has contributed to the HR profession as a DE&I panelist, podcast speaker, DallasHR education committee member and HR Southwest conference volunteer.

    Leann holds a Bachelor of Business Administration from Stephen F. Austin State University and has been certified as an SPHR and SHRM-SCP professional since 2009.

    Samantha Dwinell

    Human Capital Operating Partner
    Blue Wolf Capital

    Read Bio

    Bio:

    Sam is an accomplished global human resources leader with demonstrated success in delivering business results across private and public organizations in diverse industries. Over the past two decades, she has become a trusted business partner and member of the senior leadership team, driving significant changes and delivering innovative solutions to transform organizations. As a recognized leader, Sam received OnCon’s Top 50 CHRO Award in 2023.

    Sam currently serves as a Human Capital Operating Partner for Blue Wolf Capital—a mid-sized private equity firm—where she specializes in building strong leadership teams, stewarding performance-driven cultures and strengthening HR practices across the entire portfolio of Blue Wolf companies. Previously, Sam served as interim Chief People Officer for Stream Realty Partners—a national commercial real estate firm—and Chief People Officer for Sevita—a $2B PE-owned healthcare provider. At Sevita, Sam significantly improved employee engagement and enhanced recruiting efficiency by refreshing the company’s values and restructuring the talent acquisition function. She also established repeatable M&A processes that enabled the company to successfully integrate over two dozen acquisitions.

    Prior to Sevita, Sam spent almost 20 years with Texas Instruments—a publicly traded global semiconductor company—culminating as the Vice President of Talent Management. She was instrumental in creating an enterprise-wide talent review process and establishing TI’s first people analytics function, which leveraged HR data and analytics to improve key business outcomes. Sam also designed a high-potential talent development program that won a Brandon Hall Award for Best Results of a Learning Program.

    Throughout her career, Sam has been a passionate champion for diversity and inclusion. At Sevita, she launched the company’s first diversity, equity, inclusion and belonging initiatives, which were recognized for three consecutive years by Forbes magazine. At TI, she transformed the diversity office into a key partner inside the talent management team, leading to a more inclusive company culture and increasing the representation of women in senior leadership roles.

    Sam earned a master’s degree in Management and Organization Behavior from Benedictine University and a master’s degree in Human Resources and Industrial Relations from the University of Illinois. She is actively involved in multiple boards and industry organizations, including the Industrial Advisory Council at UT-Dallas’s Jindal School of Management, the Board and Investment Committee for the Texas Women’s Foundation, the Miami University Economics Advisory Board and previously on the Catalyst, Inc. Board of Advisors.

    Keith Pearson

    Chairman
    Pearson International

    Read Bio

    Bio:

    As founding partner, chief executive officer and chairman of Pearson Partners International, Keith Pearson manages the firm’s strategic direction and operation. He specializes in securing C-Suite leaders for Fortune 1000 and private equity portfolio companies. Keith’s commitment to flexible, responsive client service has set the companywide standard of excellence that is Pearson Partners’ hallmark. His deep knowledge of the CEO, financial officer and private equity leadership roles has made him an invaluable partner to his clients, whose repeat business is a testament to his unique ability to understand business needs, develop creative solutions and quickly source top talent for the executive suite.

    Keith and Bob Pearson launched Pearson Partners International in 2002 as an executive search firm initially serving the leveraged buyout community and have since led the company through twenty years of growth and diversification. Today, Pearson Partners is a global, full-service executive search and leadership consulting firm serving businesses across a broad range of industries while offering a full complement of professional development and coaching services.

    Before founding Pearson Partners International, Keith was a managing director with Whitehouse & Pimms in the consulting and technology sectors and with TMP Worldwide Executive Search as a principal in the professional services practice. He began his executive search career as a search consultant with Merritt, Hawkins and Associates.

    Keith serves as the Chair of the Dean’s Advisory Council of the Naveen Jindal School of Management, an Ex Officio Member of the Executive Board, and as a member of the Human Resource Management advisory board at UT Dallas. He is a member and previous chairman of the board of trustees of The Leukemia & Lymphoma Society, North Texas Chapter, where he is a longtime member of its Team in Training. Previously, he served in an advisory capacity on the Association of Executive Search and Leadership Consultants (AESC) Council of the Americas. Keith earned a Bachelor of Business Administration in management from The University of North Texas and a master’s degree in business administration from The University of Texas at Dallas. He is an avid runner and cyclist who enjoys competing in ultrathons and off-road races as well as traveling with his wife Stephanie and their three children.

    Chief People Officer
    Es Vedra Cinemas

    Read Bio

    Bio:

    With nearly two decades of experience in human resources, people leadership, and cultural development, Celi Quintero is a passionate advocate for creating thriving workplaces. As a member of the University of Texas Dallas Human Resources Advisory Council, she brings a wealth of expertise and a deep commitment to fostering inclusive and high-performing organizations.

    Celi’s journey to C-suite leadership was anything but conventional. She began her career as a stylist, where she spent countless hours building connections, listening to clients’ stories, and offering guidance—an experience that ignited her passion for people and ultimately led to a career in HR. With a Bachelor of Science in Management from the University of Phoenix, she quickly ascended through strategic HR roles across industries including commercial construction, retail, and hospitality.

    Today, as Chief Human Resources Officer at Es Vedra Cinemas, Celi is at the forefront of shaping a people-first culture within a dynamic entertainment company. She plays a key role in driving organizational growth, employee engagement, and operational excellence, ensuring that the company’s workforce remains its greatest asset.

    Celi’s leadership philosophy is rooted in authenticity, empathy, and a relentless drive to empower individuals and teams. She is honored to serve on the advisory board, where she shares her insights and experiences to help shape the future of HR leadership.

    Triumph Financial

    Read Bio

    Bio:

    Mario serves as Senior Vice President of Human Resources at Triumph Financial, where he advises key executives, including the Chief Revenue Officer and Presidents of Insurance Services and Factoring. His leadership has been instrumental in facilitating a strategic priority process that shaped a new organizational vision and reinvigorated a customer-centric culture. Mario also implemented performance and reward structures that led to significant increases in employee retention and organizational performance.

He has been a certified coach with the International Coaching Federation and holds a Senior Professional in Human Resources (SPHR) designation.
